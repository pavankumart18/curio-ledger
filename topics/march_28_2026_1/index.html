Executive Summary

The Indian Air Force (IAF), officially designated as the Bhāratīya Vāyu Senā, functions as the primary instrument of Indian kinetic and diplomatic statecraft. Operating under a complex dual-threat paradigm along its western (Pakistan) and northern/eastern (China) frontiers, the force ensures sovereign airspace security and projects overwhelming aerial capabilities.

Beyond core kinetic warfare, the IAF provides vital strategic airlift, high-altitude logistics, and Humanitarian Assistance and Disaster Relief (HADR) across the Indo-Pacific region.

⚖️ The Philosophical Ethos

The IAF's motto, "Touch the Sky with Glory" (Nabhaḥ Spr̥śaṁ Dīptam), originates from Chapter 11, Verse 24 of the Bhagavad Gita. It describes Lord Krishna's terrifying, overwhelming cosmic form. This strategically aligns the IAF's doctrine with the projection of awe-inspiring, paralyzing aerospace power.

🧑‍✈️ The 45-Degree Salute

Redesigned in 2006, the IAF salute places the open palm at exactly a 45-degree angle to the ground. This biomechanical trajectory physically manifests progress towards the skies (aircraft takeoff), distinguishing it from the Army (forward-facing) and Navy (downward-facing).

Historical Genesis

From a colonial auxiliary unit to a network-centric aerospace power, the IAF's trajectory is deeply entwined with India's strategic autonomy.

1932

Establishment & Early Skepticism

Founded on October 8 with 6 RAF-trained Indian officers and 29 'havai sepoys' operating four Westland Wapiti biplanes. Survived early colonial skepticism regarding Indian aviation capabilities.

1939 - 1945

Crucible of WWII & the "Royal" Prefix

Indian pilots played crucial roles halting Japanese advances in Arakan and Imphal. Awarded the prefix "Royal" in 1945, later dropped when India became a Republic in 1950.

1954 & The Great Conflicts

Indigenization & Decisive Warfare

Air Marshal Subroto Mukherjee became the first Indian Chief of Air Staff (1954). The IAF subsequently proved vital in the 1965 war and the decisive 1971 liberation of Bangladesh.

1999

Kargil War (Op Safed Sagar)

Executed unprecedented high-altitude precision strikes, adapting traditional jets for extreme elevations, proving air power viability in mountainous terrain.

Organizational Architecture

Commanded by the Chief of the Air Staff (Air Chief Marshal Amar Preet Singh as of 2025), the IAF maintains agility across a vast geography via 5 Operational and 2 Functional Commands.

Western Air Command (WAC)

HQ: New Delhi

Largest command (16 bases). Crucial for countering Pakistan and managing contested northern borders.

Eastern Air Command (EAC)

HQ: Shillong, Meghalaya

Primary bulwark against Chinese aerial incursions along the Line of Actual Control (LAC) with 15 bases.

Central Air Command (CAC)

HQ: Prayagraj, UP

Strategic depth command; major hub for transport, logistics, and heavy airlift.

Southern Air Command (SAC)

HQ: Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala

Protects IOR shipping routes and conducts maritime strike ops across 11 bases.

South Western Air Command

HQ: Gandhinagar, Gujarat

Frontline defense against Pakistan in the western desert and maritime borders.

Functional Commands

Training (TC): Bengaluru. Foundational & flight training.

Maintenance (MC): Nagpur. Logistics, repair, and supply chain.

🌎

Extraterritorial Reach: The IAF operates its only foreign air base, the Farkhor Air Base in Tajikistan, providing strategic depth in Central Asia.

Strategic Procurement Vectors

Indigenous Fighters (Tejas & AMCA)

Bedrock

141 Tejas Mk1A ordered (AESA radar, EW suites). The 5th Gen stealth AMCA program received CCS approval in 2024, targeting induction in the 2030s.

High-End Combat (Rafale & Su-30MKI)

Spearhead

36 Dassault Rafales form the elite strike force. The 265 Su-30MKIs are undergoing the "Super Sukhoi" upgrade. MRFA program (114 jets) remains active.

Transport & Autonomous Systems

Logistics/Future

56 C-295s inducting (40 built in India by Tata). 2025's "Unmanned Force Plan" initiated $4.2B MQ-9B drone deal and Ghatak UCAV development.

Contemporary Operations (2025-2026)

Recent high-stakes environments demonstrate a shift toward multi-domain operations, precision strikes, and electronic warfare.

May 2025

Operation Sindoor

A calibrated kinetic strike targeting militant infrastructure in PoK and Punjab following terror provocations.

  • ✓ Employed air-launched BrahMos and SCALP-EG missiles.
  • ✓ S-400 "Sudarshan" neutralized retaliatory strikes, destroying 5-6 adversary fighters and a spy plane ~300km deep.
  • ✓ Navigated intense Info-Warfare (deepfakes, false AI footage) maintaining strict operational discipline.
Mar-Apr 2025

Operation Brahma & EW

Massive HADR mission post-Myanmar earthquake (airlifting 656 MT relief via C-130J/C-17). Pilots faced intense GPS Spoofing cyberattacks, forcing reliance on internal navigation systems—highlighting contested peacetime airspace.

Global Interoperability

Diplomacy & Exercises

Hosting Tarang Shakti (largest multilateral drill), participating in Ex Garuda (France), Cope India (US), and the Feb 2026 In-Situ Joint Air Exercise with Thailand near the Malacca Strait.
