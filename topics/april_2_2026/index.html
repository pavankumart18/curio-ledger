Humanity's Return to Deep Space.

Artemis II is the first crewed flight test of the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and the Orion spacecraft. Paving the way for lunar surface missions, this approximately 10-day flight will test foundational human deep space exploration capabilities.

Launch & Earth Orbit

The SLS rocket launches Orion from Kennedy Space Center. After reaching initial Earth orbit, the Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage (ICPS) fires to raise the orbit, testing life support systems close to home.

Hardware Capabilities

The backbone of the Artemis missions lies in the unprecedented power of the Space Launch System (SLS) and the advanced technology of the Orion crew module. This section compares key metrics of the launch vehicle against historical benchmarks to provide scale and context to the engineering achievement.

🚀

Space Launch System (Block 1)

Producing 8.8 million pounds of maximum thrust, the SLS is the most powerful rocket ever launched by NASA. It utilizes four RS-25 engines (heritage Space Shuttle hardware) and two massive solid rocket boosters.

🛰️

Orion & ESM

The Orion crew module, powered by the European Service Module (ESM) which provides air, water, power, and propulsion, is designed to sustain humans in deep space farther and longer than any previous spacecraft.
