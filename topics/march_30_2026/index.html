Anatomy of Emotional States

Emotions are rarely caused by a single hormone; they are cocktails. This matrix breaks down the four primary physiological states discussed in the report, highlighting which chemicals are surging and which are depleted during these experiences.

Stress & Anxiety

⚠️

An evolutionary survival mechanism that goes into overdrive in modern environments.

  • Cortisol ↑ Chronically High
  • Adrenaline ↑ Spiking
  • Serotonin ↓ Suppressed
Impact: Increased heart rate, hyper-vigilance, digestive issues, sleep disruption.

Depression

🌧️

A complex state of neurological depletion, often following prolonged chronic stress.

  • Serotonin ↓ Severely Low
  • Dopamine ↓ Low
  • Cortisol ↕ Dysregulated
Impact: Lethargy, lack of motivation (anhedonia), disrupted sleep architecture, cognitive fog.

Love & Attachment

❤️

A powerful physiological state designed to promote pair-bonding and social survival.

  • Oxytocin ↑ Very High
  • Dopamine ↑ High
  • Cortisol ↓ Lowered
Impact: Feelings of safety, reduced pain perception, intense focus on partner, stress buffering.

Optimal Balance

⚖️

Homeostasis. The body is responsive to challenges but quickly returns to baseline.

  • Serotonin ✓ Stable
  • Dopamine ✓ Regulated
  • Cortisol ↺ Normal Rhythm
Impact: Emotional resilience, steady energy, clear cognition, healthy sleep-wake cycle.
